\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n pu\xc5\xa1\xc5\xa1u\xe1\xb8\xb3u - to explain\n (CAD P 545)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Most probably a loanword from Aramaic (AHw. 841 pa\xc5\xa1\xc4\x81qu D mng. 2, CAD P 545, Cherry 2017:205f.). As argued in Cherry 2017:206, the unusual correspondence Aramaic \xc5\xa1 ~ Assyrian \xc5\xa1 (instead of the expected s) may be due to the influence of pa\xc5\xa1\xc4\x81\xe1\xb8\xb3u \xe2\x80\x98to be narrow\xe2\x80\x99.\n
