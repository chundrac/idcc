\n \n PWS\n \n
\n
\n \n *gann-, *gann-at- - garden\n (Kogan 2015: 208)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n If the Geez word gannat \xe2\x80\x98garden\xe2\x80\x99 is autochthonous, the present lexeme cannot be considered a specific feature of PCS. A reflex of *gann- \xe2\x80\x98garden\xe2\x80\x99 is probably to be seen in Amh. g\xc3\xa4nn\xc3\xa4n\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to flourish, to grow well (plants, trees), to be abundant (vegetation)\xe2\x80\x99. Relatively broad presence of the reflexes in ESA obviates the necessity of postulating a \xe2\x80\x9cNorthern\xe2\x80\x9d borrowing in Ethiopian (and an Aramaic borrowing in Arabic) (Kogan 2015: 208-209)\n
