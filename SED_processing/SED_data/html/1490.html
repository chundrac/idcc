Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*prr - to yawn (reflexive-intensive stem)\n (Kogan 2015:568)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Leslau is likely correct to derive Proto-MSA *prr (reflexive-intensive stem) \xe2\x80\x98to yawn\xe2\x80\x99 from an original meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto divide,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cto separate,\xe2\x80\x9d eventually compatible with PS *prr represented by Akk. par\xc4\x81ru (D) \xe2\x80\x98to break up, to disperse,\xe2\x80\x99 naparruru \xe2\x80\x98to become crushed, dispersed, separated\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD P 161), Ugr. prr \xe2\x80\x98to break\xe2\x80\x99 (DUL 681), Hbr. prr \xe2\x80\x98to break, to destroy\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 974), Arb. \xcb\x80afarra ra\xcb\x80sahu bi-s-sayfi = \xc5\xa1aqqaqahu wa-falaqahu (TA 13 318), Gez. farra \xe2\x80\x98to shell, to husk\xe2\x80\x99 (CDG 166), Tna. f\xc3\xa4r\xc3\xa4r\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to dissolve flour in water\xe2\x80\x99 (TED 2659). For a closer semantic parallelism cf. Tna. f\xc3\xa4r\xc3\xa4r b\xc3\xa4l\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to be opened wide (eyes due to astonishment)\xe2\x80\x99 (TED 2659). (Kogan 2015:568)\n
