*na\xcb\x81l- - shoe, sandal\n (Kogan 2015: 215)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

PCS *na\xcb\x81l- \xe2\x80\x98shoe\xe2\x80\x99 could be plausibly connected with *n\xcb\x81l \xe2\x80\x98to lock, to secure,\xe2\x80\x99 but this verbal root is too sparsely attested: Hbr. n\xcb\x81l \xe2\x80\x98to secure, to bolt\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 705), man\xcb\x81\xc5\xabl, min\xcb\x81\xc4\x81l \xe2\x80\x98bar, bolt\xe2\x80\x99 (ibid. 602). One cannot exclude that *na\xcb\x81l- represents a PCS replacement of a more ancient common designation of \xe2\x80\x9cshoe\xe2\x80\x9d *\xc5\x9da\xcb\x80n-.
