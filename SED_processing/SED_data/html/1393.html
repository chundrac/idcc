\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n ta\xe1\xb8\xaban\xc4\x81tu - help, favour\n (AHw 1301, CAD T 40)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

Probably an early WS loanword reconstructible as *ta-\xcb\x81\xc4\x81n-at- \xe2\x80\x98help, support\xe2\x80\x99. This rather plausible etymology is not free of difficulties: 1) Early WS loanwords are rather uncommon in texts from the core Babylonian area. 2) The root\xc2\xa0\xcb\x81wn is not so common in the NWS languages. 3) The morphological shape\xc2\xa0*ta-\xcb\x81\xc4\x81n-at- is not the expected one in the present case. See EDA II for details.
