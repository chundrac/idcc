*\xe1\xb8\xb3(\xca\xb7)Vy\xe1\xb9\xa3-, *k(\xca\xb7)V\xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb9\xa3- - joint, point of connection between bones\n (SED I No. 172 )\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Attested in South-Semitic area only. Meanings in Arb., Gez. and Soq. rather diverse; S. Eth. forms may be of a different origin.\xc2\xa0

\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n kim\xe1\xb9\xa3u, kin\xe1\xb9\xa3u, ki\xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb9\xa3u - knee, shin, calf of the leg\n (CAD k 373, AHw 478)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

With k\xc2\xad < *\xe1\xb8\xb3\xc2\xad by dissimilation of two \xc2\xabemphatic\xc2\xbb consonants.\xc2\xa0

\r\n\r\n

The form ki\xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb9\xa3u would exactly correspond to Gez.\xc2\xa0\xe1\xb8\xb3\xca\xb7\xc9\x99y\xe1\xb9\xa3, \xe1\xb8\xb3\xca\xb7\xc9\x99\xe1\xb9\xa3 if not for the fact that it can hardly be separated from the Akk forms with \xc2\xadm\xc2\xad and \xc2\xadn\xc2\xad, of which kim\xe1\xb9\xa3u looks a primary one (cf. also kam\xc4\x81su, kam\xc4\x81\xe1\xb9\xa3u \xe2\x80\x98to squat, kneel\xe2\x80\x99).\xc2\xa0
