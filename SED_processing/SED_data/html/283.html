*sbb - to turn\n (Kogan 2015: 304)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n may be related to Arb. sibb- \xe2\x80\x98turban,\xe2\x80\x99 sabab- \xe2\x80\x98cord, rope,\xe2\x80\x99 sab\xc4\xabb- \xe2\x80\x98a lock of hair\xe2\x80\x99, Tgr. \xc5\xa1\xc3\xa4ba\xc5\xa1\xc3\xa4b\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to put in folds (garment)\xe2\x80\x99, Tna. s\xc3\xa4bs\xc3\xa4b\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to tuck up, to roll up\xe2\x80\x99, Amh. \xc5\xa1\xc3\xa4b\xc3\xa4\xc5\xa1\xc5\xa1\xc3\xa4b\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to wrinkle, to pleat\xe2\x80\x99, although Akk. \xc5\xa1ibbu \xe2\x80\x98belt, girdle\xe2\x80\x99 compared in CDG 483 makes this identification problematic (Akk. \xc5\xa1 does not regularly correspond to Hbr. s). Sab. s\xe2\x82\x83bb \xe2\x80\x98to surround an enemy\xe2\x80\x99 would provide a more direct cognate, but the meaning of this military term is rather obscure. Whether Gez. \xcb\x80asb\xc4\x81b \xe2\x80\x98guards, sentinels\xe2\x80\x99 goes back to this root is, contra CDG 483 and LLA 359, completely uncertain\r\n(hapax legomenon in Ja 631:32\xe2\x80\x9234) is quite obscure\r\n(Kogan 2015: 304-305)\n
