\n \n Soqotri\n \n
\n
\n \n am - big (f.)\n (LS 63)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

In the Vienna Corpus the concept \xe2\x80\x9cbig\xe2\x80\x9d is expressed by the suppletive combination of eb for the masculine vs. am for the feminine. Numerous illustrations can be found in D. M\xc3\xbcller\xe2\x80\x99s special note on the subject (M\xc3\xbcller 1909:347-351), such as bil\xc3\xa2d \xca\x94\xc3\xa9eb/\xe1\xb8\xa5ad\xc3\xadboh \xca\x94am \xe2\x80\x98die gro\xc3\x9fe Stadt\xe2\x80\x99. In the dialect of the Da\xe2\x80\x99rho tribe, upon which CSOL I is based, this usage is obsolete: the adjectives am and eb are restricted to the comparative expressions like m\xe1\xb8\xa5\xc3\xa1mmad eb \xca\x95an \xc3\xa1\xe1\xb8\xa5mad \xe2\x80\x98Muhammed is bigger than Ahmad\xe2\x80\x99 or f\xc3\xa1\xe1\xb9\xadma am \xca\x95an s\xc3\xa1l\xe2\x80\x99wa \xe2\x80\x98Fatima is bigger than Selwa\xe2\x80\x99, but still current in their original basic function in the Western Soqotri dialects, according to a personal communication from Miranda Morris.
\r\n(Kogan 2015: 487)
