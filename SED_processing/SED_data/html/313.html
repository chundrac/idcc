*y\xe1\xb9\xafn - to wear out, grow old\n (Ginsberg 1970:103, Kogan 2015: 316)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Origin uncertain. Comparison with Arb. \xcb\x80sn \xe2\x80\x98to be altered, long standing, rancid (water)\xe2\x80\x99 and snn (IV) \xe2\x80\x98to be advanced in age\xe2\x80\x99 suggested in Tropper 2000:109, albeit semantically attractive, is undermined by the irregular phonological correspondence. DRS 658 compares Arb. \xe1\xb9\xafnn (IV) \xe2\x80\x98to become wasted and worn out (an old man),\xe2\x80\x99 \xe1\xb9\xafinn- \xe2\x80\x98dry herbage\xe2\x80\x99. Cf. also Arb. w\xe1\xb9\xafn (X) \xe2\x80\x98to become numerous, abundant\xe2\x80\x99 (Kogan 2015: 316)\n
