*bi/ba - in; by means of (instrumental)\n (Arakelova 2001:31, Kogan 2015: 77)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n An eventual derivation from either *bayt- \xe2\x80\x98house\xe2\x80\x99 or *bw\xcb\x80 \xe2\x80\x98to come\xe2\x80\x99 is not to be excluded (cf. Voigt 1999:37, 39\xe2\x80\x9240, Bla\xc5\xbeek 2007:31).
\r\nIn several languages (Ugr., Pho., Heb., Arm., Har., Gur.) this preposition has a meaning \xe2\x80\x9cfrom\xe2\x80\x9d, nearly opposite to the locative meaning \xe2\x80\x9cin\xe2\x80\x9d. These forms could be ascribed to a separate homonymous preposition which could be united with ESA forms of the type bn with the same meaning (Sab. bn, Min. bn, Qat. bn). However, it is essential that Akk. ina possesses both of these meanings (Arakelova 2001:33).\n

\n \n Tigre\n \n
\n
\n \n \xcb\x80\xc9\x99b/b\xc9\x99- - in, through, with, by\n (WTS 365)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Just as in Tna., there are two reflexes of the preposition *bi- in Tgr.: \xcb\x80\xc9\x99b with the initial glottal stop and b\xc9\x99- without it. In contrast to Tna., where \xcb\x80ab and b\xc9\x99 have different meanings (locative and instrumental respectively), Tgr. forms are phonetic variants of a single preposition.\n
