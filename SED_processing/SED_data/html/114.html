*bi/a\xc5\x9dar- - meat\n (SED I No. 41; Kogan 2011: 214; Kogan 2015: 112)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The meanings \xe2\x80\x98child\xe2\x80\x99 (the only one preserved in Akk. and Pun.) and \xe2\x80\x98mankind\xe2\x80\x99 (preserved in Hbr., Bib., Arb. and Min.) may be explained by the notion of common origin, or consanguinity, associated with flesh. \xc2\xabAnd our arm will not be upon him, - Judas says about Joseph to his brothers, - for he is our brother, our flesh (b\xc9\x99\xc5\x9d\xc4\x81r\xc4\x93n\xc5\xab)\xc2\xbb (Gen. 37:27); for a similar semantic connection, another word for \xe2\x80\x98flesh\xe2\x80\x99, \xc5\xa1\xc9\x99\xcb\x80\xc4\x93r, rendering the same idea of consanguinity, see Lev. 18:12: \xc2\xabDo not disclose bareness of your father\xe2\x80\x99s sister: she is of the same blood (\xc5\xa1\xc9\x99\xcb\x80\xc4\x93r, lit. flesh) as your father\xc2\xbb. The stem *bi\xc5\x9dr-\xc2\xad reconstructed on Akk., Arm., and probably MSA; *ba\xc5\x9dar-\xc2\xad, on Hbr., Arb., and Eth.\n

\n \n Arabic\n \n
\n
\n \n ba\xc5\xa1ar- - epidermis; mankind\n (BK 1 129, Lane 208)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n ba\xc5\xa1ar- was relegated to the meanings \xe2\x80\x9cepidermis\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cmankind\xe2\x80\x9d under the impact of la\xe1\xb8\xa5m- (originally, \xe2\x80\x9cfood\xe2\x80\x9d in general) (Kogan 2015: 112).\r\nFor the meaning \xe2\x80\x98mankind\xe2\x80\x99 see also bs\xe2\x82\x82r in Min and Sab\r\nAlso ba\xc5\xa1\xc4\x81rat-\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98beauty of shapes, body\xe2\x80\x99 [ibid.].\r\nNote the meaning \xe2\x80\x98skin\xe2\x80\x99 attested in Arb. and MSA, which could suggest the PS meaning \xe2\x80\x98flesh with skin\xe2\x80\x99, if not for MSA parallels suspect of being Arb. loanwords, in which case a meaning shift in Arb. is to be regarded as an Arb. semantic\r\ninnovation.\n
