\n \n PES\n \n
\n
\n \n *wald-/*lVd- - son\n (Kogan 2015:458)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n \xe2\x80\x9cThe original meaning of Proto-EthS *wald- (and *lVd-) \xe2\x80\x98son\xe2\x80\x99 was probably \xe2\x80\x9cboy,\xe2\x80\x9d as is the case of its immediate parallels in Hebrew (y\xc3\xa4l\xc3\xa4d, HALOT 412) and Arabic (walad-, Lane 2966). All these terms are derived from PS *wld \xe2\x80\x98to bear\xe2\x80\x99, widely attested both withing and outside EthS (SED I No. 80v). Lack of lexical distinction between the meanings \xe2\x80\x9cson\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cboy\xe2\x80\x9d/\xe2\x80\x9dchild\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x94 similar to b\xc4\x93n vs. y\xc3\xa4l\xc3\xa4d in Hebrew \xe2\x80\x94 observable throughout EthS is atypical for Semitic languages and might be due to the substratum influence.\xe2\x80\x9d\n
