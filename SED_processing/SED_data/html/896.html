\n \n *ng\xc5\x9d - to push, to press, to drive to work\n (CDG 393; Appleyard 1977: 51; Kogan 2015: 457)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Probably related to Akk. nag\xc4\x81\xc5\xa1u \xe2\x80\x98to leave, to go away\xe2\x80\x99, in which case the transitive meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto drive\xe2\x80\x9d may be regarded as a\r\ncommon innovation of CS/EthS. Conversely, there is hardly any connection between\r\nthis root and Mhr. n\xc9\x99g\xc5\xab\xc5\x9d \xe2\x80\x98to shake milk for butter\xe2\x80\x99, Jib. ng\xc9\x94\xc5\x9d id.\n
