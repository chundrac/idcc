*\xe1\xb9\xaf\xcc\xa3wy - to smell\n (Kogan 2015:575\xe2\x80\x93576)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Possible cognates of Proto-MSA *\xe1\xb9\xaf\xcc\xa3wy \xe2\x80\x98to smell\xe2\x80\x99 are extensively discussed in SED I No. 286. From the semantic point of view, the closest parallels are Ugr. \xe1\xb9\xaf\xcc\xa3\xcb\x80u \xe2\x80\x98exhalation, secretion\xe2\x80\x99 (DUL 1007) and Gez. \xe1\xb9\xa3i\xcb\x80 \xe2\x80\x98bad smell.\xe2\x80\x99 Also remarkable are Hbr. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc5\x8d\xcb\x80\xc4\x81, \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc4\x93\xcb\x80\xc4\x81 \xe2\x80\x98excrement,\xe2\x80\x99 \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc4\x93(\xcb\x80) \xe2\x80\x98filth\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 992) and Gez. \xe1\xb9\xa3i\xcb\x80at \xe2\x80\x98rot, filth, stench, excrement\xe2\x80\x99 (CDG 567). (Kogan 2015:575-576)\n
