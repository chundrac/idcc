Proto-MSA
\n

*mi-\xe1\xb8\xb3raf-at- - shoulder blade\n (Kogan 2015: 562)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Proto-MSA *mi-\xe1\xb8\xb3raf-at- \xe2\x80\x98shoulder blade\xe2\x80\x99 is undoubtedly derived from the verbal root *\xe1\xb8\xb3rp \xe2\x80\x98to scratch off,\xe2\x80\x99 preserved in Arb. qrf \xe2\x80\x98to peel off the bark of atree\xe2\x80\x99 (LA 9 334) and Gez. \xe1\xb8\xb3arafa \xe2\x80\x98to peel off\xe2\x80\x99 (CDG 441, with cognates in other EthS). Indeed, as pointed out by T. M. Johnstone, the shoulder blade is \xe2\x80\x9cused as a broom to clean out a pen-cave\xe2\x80\x9d (ML 235). For similar \xe2\x80\x9cinstrumental\xe2\x80\x9d designations of this body part cf. Akk. naglabu (CAD N1 119,1464 AHw. 711, Streck 2002:231), Latin scapula (WH II 489\xe2\x80\x92490), English shoulder blade, Russian \xd0\xbb\xd0\xbe\xd0\xbf\xd0\xb0\xd1\x82\xd0\xba\xd0\xb0 (diminutive from \xd0\xbb\xd0\xbe\xd0\xbf\xd0\xb0\xd1\x82\xd0\xb0 \xe2\x80\x98shovel,\xe2\x80\x99 Vasmer II 519). Leslau\xe2\x80\x99s comparison with Tna. gw\xc9\x99rb\xc9\x99tta \xe2\x80\x98rump\xe2\x80\x99 (TED 2262) and Amh. \xc7\xaf\xc3\xa4rba \xe2\x80\x98back\xe2\x80\x99 (AED 1859) can hardly be accepted. (Kogan 2015:562)\n
