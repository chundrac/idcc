\n \xe2\x80\x9cIn continental MSA, *kalb- has acquired the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cwolf\xe2\x80\x9d, but continues to mean \xe2\x80\x9cdog\xe2\x80\x9d both as an independent lexeme and in combination with *ma-b\xcb\x81al- (originally, \xe2\x80\x9csomething owned, domestic\xe2\x80\x9d, cf. Mhr. m\xc4\x81b\xc3\xa1yl \xe2\x80\x98owned\xe2\x80\x99 ML 41, Soq m\xc9\x99\xcc\x81b\xe1\xb8\xa5\xc9\x99l\xca\xbc \xe2\x80\x98esclave\xe2\x80\x99 in LS 91). (Kogan 2015:533, fn. 1401)\n

\n \n Soqotri\n \n
\n
\n \n ka\xe1\xb8\xb7b - dog, wolf\n (LS 218, CSOL II 499)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

\xe2\x80\x9cThe Soqotri lexeme may be a comparatively late Arabism as dogs are unknown on the island\xe2\x80\x9d. (Kogan 2015:533fn). It is worth noting that the informants of the Da\xe2\x80\x99rho tribe pronounce it with the \xe2\x80\x9cdark\xe2\x80\x9d \xe1\xb8\xb7 which is characteristic of the autochthonous Soqotri vocabulary and must point to a rather deep degree of adaption. (Kogan 2015:ibid.).\xc2\xa0Contrast the \xe2\x80\x9cold\xe2\x80\x9d, now practically extinct genuine Soqotri realization of this lexeme with the \xe2\x80\x9cdark\xe2\x80\x9d \xe1\xb8\xb7, and the recent Arabic loanword ka\xc4\xbeb in 1:52 (CSOL II 499).
