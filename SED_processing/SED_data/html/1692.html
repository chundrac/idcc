\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n \xe1\xb9\xa3allalu - a nocturnal bird\n (CAD \xe1\xb9\xa2 73, AHw. 1077)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n SB. Known from lexical lists (= Sum. N\xc3\x81.A MUSEN, Akk. i\xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb9\xa3\xc5\xabr m\xc5\xab\xc5\xa1i \xe2\x80\x98a night-bird\xe2\x80\x99) and bird-omina of summa \xc4\x81lu, identified with long-eared owl in Salonen 1973 168, 232, 262. Both dictionaries derive it from the verb \xe1\xb9\xa3al\xc4\x81lu \xe2\x80\x98to sleep\xe2\x80\x99 (according to Salonen, \xe2\x80\x98(w\xc3\xa4hrend des Tages) schlafender Vogel\xe2\x80\x99) which is not unlikely in view of the fact that Sum. N\xc3\x81.(A.)MUSEN (N\xc3\x81 = er\xc5\xa1u \xe2\x80\x98bed\xe2\x80\x99, n\xc3\xa2lu \xe2\x80\x98to sleep\xe2\x80\x99) is attested already in Presargonic lexical lists. A folk-etymological reinterpretation of a Common Semitic bird name cannot be ruled out, however.\n
