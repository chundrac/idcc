\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n arkabu - (kind of) bat\n (CAD I 177, AHw. 67, Civil 1984a)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Var. , irkabu, argabu.
\r\nProbably related to PS *\xcb\x81akbar- \xe2\x80\x98mouse, jerboa\xe2\x80\x99 with a meaning shift and metathesis. The phonetic shape of the Akk. term and its meaning have been convincingly established in a special study Civil 1984a (contrast \xe2\x80\x98a bird\xe2\x80\x99 in CAD I 177, \xe2\x80\x98Taube\xe2\x80\x99 in AHw. 67; note that the translation \xe2\x80\x98bat\xe2\x80\x99 is present in some earlier studies such as Landsberger 1934 97, Biggs 1967 25).\n
