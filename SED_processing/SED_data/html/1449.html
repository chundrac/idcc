*nV\xe1\xb9\xaf\xe1\xb8\xb3- - weapon\n (Kogan 2015: 300, 343)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Hbr. n\xc3\xa4\xc5\xa1\xc3\xa4\xe1\xb8\xb3 is usually compared with Arb. nsq \xe2\x80\x98to put in order\xe2\x80\x99 Gez. nasa\xe1\xb8\xb3a \xe2\x80\x98to arrange in order, join closely\xe2\x80\x99, which implies a semantic derivation from \xe2\x80\x9corder of battle\xe2\x80\x9d to \xe2\x80\x9cwar,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cwarfare,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cweapons\xe2\x80\x9d (Kopf 1976:206\xe2\x80\x92208). But CDG 403 relates them to Akk \xc5\xa1utassu\xe1\xb8\xb3u \xe2\x80\x98to put in order, to make ready, to prepare\xe2\x80\x99. And phonologically problematic - reliable examples of PS *\xc5\xa1 > Ugr. \xe1\xb9\xaf are few (possible cases: Tropper 2000:109\xe2\x80\x92111).\n
