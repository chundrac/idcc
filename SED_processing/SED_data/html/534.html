PS
\n
\n

*bi\xcb\x80r-, *bu\xcb\x80r(-at)- - well, source of water\n (Fronzaroli 1971: 611, 632, 640; Kogan 2011: 192)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

One can easily surmise that the u-forms have secondarily emerged under the influence of b, at least some of them are quite ancient and may reflect a secondary lexicalization of the two variants as early as in PS (perhaps with semantic specification \xe2\x80\x9cwell\xe2\x80\x9d vs. \xe2\x80\x9cpit\xe2\x80\x9d). Note that for Fronzaroli 1971, 611 *bu\xcb\x80r- is diachronically primary with respect to a more innovative *bi\xcb\x80r-, which is hard to explain in phonological terms.
