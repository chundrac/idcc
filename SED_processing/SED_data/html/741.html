*mVr(V)r-at- - gall, gall bladder\n (SED I No. 188; Kogan 2011: 219)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Most Sem. forms with a reduplicated second radical may be derived after various deverbal paterns from *mrr \xe2\x80\x98to be bitter\xe2\x80\x99. However, the fact that besides nominal forms meaning \xe2\x80\x98bile, gall, gall-bladder\xe2\x80\x99, there are also terms throughout Sem. meaning only \xe2\x80\x98gall-bladder\xe2\x80\x99, and even \xe2\x80\x98bladder\xe2\x80\x99 (in Gur). and \xe2\x80\x98stomach\xe2\x80\x99 (in Soq.), makes one doubt that this anatomical term derived from the verb \xe2\x80\x98to be bitter\xe2\x80\x99; a contamination with this verb (especially in case of Arm. and Hbr. pB. \xe2\x80\x98poison\xe2\x80\x99) is very likely.
