*pa\xcb\x81m- - leg\n (SED I No. 207)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

\xe2\x80\x9cThe semantic agreement between continental MSA and Ugaritic-Phoenician (...) might suggest that *pa\xcb\x81m- was the main designation of \xe2\x80\x9cfoot\xe2\x80\x9d already in PS. If this assumption is correct, the meaning \xe2\x80\x9chip\xe2\x80\x9d, normal for Akk. p\xc4\x93mu, must be innovative. On the other hand, the meaning of Soq. fa\xcb\x81m (\xe2\x80\x99upper part of the back leg of goats and sheep\xe2\x80\x99) comes notoriously close to that of the Akkadian lexeme. The same is true of the verbal root f\xcb\x81m in Arabic (\xe2\x80\x99to be thick in one\xe2\x80\x99s shanks\xe2\x80\x99). In such conditions, one cannot exclude that the meanings \xe2\x80\x9cupper leg\xe2\x80\x9d, \xe2\x80\x9chip\xe2\x80\x9d, \xe2\x80\x9cthigh\xe2\x80\x9d reflect the most primitive situation, whereas generalization to \xe2\x80\x9cfoot\xe2\x80\x9d took place independently in continental MSA and Canaanite.\xe2\x80\x9d (Kogan 2015:586-587)
\r\n
\r\nphonetic split into p\xcb\x81n \xe2\x80\x98foot\xe2\x80\x99 and p\xcb\x80amt \xe2\x80\x98times\xe2\x80\x99 in Ugaritic
