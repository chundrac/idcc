*-kVmV/-kVnV - your; you (genitive and accusative pronominal suffix, 2 pl.)\n (Kogan 2009:69)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Just as the singular forms, the plural of the pronominal suffix of the 2nd person is based on the element k- with the extension -m- or -n- combined with more or less expressed vocalic oppositions (Kogan 2009:69). The character of consonantal and vocalic oppositions in individual languages generally corresponds to the nominative forms.\n
