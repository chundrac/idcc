*\xc5\xa1\xc4\xab\xcc\x86/\xc5\xa1\xc4\xab\xcc\x86\xcb\x80/\xc5\xa1\xc4\xab\xcc\x86ya - she\n (Kogan 2009:66)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Forms with the initial sibilant, whose primary nature cannot be doubted in view of the data of non-Semitic Afroasiatic languages, are found in Akk. and, to a greater or lesser extent, in MSA. In Ug., Heb., Arb. and Sab. *\xc5\xa1- shifts to h- (Kogan 2009:66).\n

\n \n Geez\n \n
\n
\n \n y\xc9\x99\xcb\x80\xc9\x99ti - she\n (CDG 625)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n No convincing explanation of the loss of the initial *h has been suggested. The deictic extension *-t\xc4\xab has parallels in other pronominal forms (for example, Geez z\xc4\x81tti). See Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:98.\n
