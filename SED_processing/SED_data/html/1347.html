\n

*li/la - to (preposition)\n (Arakelova 2001:33, Kogan 2015:119)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n its derivation from the verbal root *wly \xe2\x80\x98to adhere, to be near\xe2\x80\x99 advocated in\r\nVoigt 1999:41 is highly unlikely \r\nIf the preposition is diachronically identical with the precative marker\r\n*li (Huehnergard 2006:16), one may\r\ntrace it back to PS, assuming an independent loss of its prepositional function\r\nin Akkadian and MSA (the l-precative is prominently attested in both). If this\r\nrelationship is rejected, the preposition *li may be regarded as a shared innovation\r\nof the CS/EthS genealogical unity (Kogan 2015: 119)\n

\n \n Ugaritic\n \n
\n
\n \n l - to; for; from\n (DUL 475)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n The presence of the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cfrom\xe2\x80\x9d, which is opposite to the basic meaning of the preposition, is noteworthy. A similar picture may be characteristic of Heb. l\xc9\x99. Compare also the combination of nearly opposite meanings \xe2\x80\x9cin\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cfrom\xe2\x80\x9d attested for PWS *bi (Arakelova 2001:35\xe2\x80\x9336).\n

\n \n Hebrew\n \n
\n
\n \n l\xc9\x99 - to, toward (preposition)\n (HALOT 507)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n It is possible that in addition to the basic meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto, for\xe2\x80\x9d this preposition also had the meaning \xe2\x80\x9c(away) from\xe2\x80\x9d. However, all the examples gathered in HALOT 507 are problematic (Arakelova 2001:35\xe2\x80\x9336). For the possibility of such a combination of meanings within a single preposition see Ug. l and PWS *bi.\n
