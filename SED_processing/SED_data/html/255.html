*ta\xe1\xb8\xa5t- - under\n (HALOT 1721\xe2\x80\x921722, CDG 573, Arakelova 2001:43, Kogan 2015:384)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n No convincing\r\ninternal etymological reconstruction suggests itself, although the similarity\r\nbetween *ta\xe1\xb8\xa5ta and the Proto-Aramaic root *n\xe1\xb8\xa5t \xe2\x80\x98to go down\xe2\x80\x99 is noteworthy. It is usually assumed that this preposition is derived from the noun \xe2\x80\x98lower part, what is beneath\xe2\x80\x99 (DNWSI 1210, CDG 573), which is not unquestionable: at least, the distribution of the preposition over languages is much broader than that of the noun (Arakelova 2001:44).\n
