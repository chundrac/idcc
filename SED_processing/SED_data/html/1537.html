*\xc5\x9d\xcc\xa3agm- - cheek\n (Kogan 2015:573)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n One is tempted to follow W. M\xc3\xbcller (apud CDG 149) who tentatively connects Proto-MSA *\xc5\x9d\xcc\xa3agm- \xe2\x80\x98cheek\xe2\x80\x99 with Gez. \xc5\x9d\xcc\xa3agama \xe2\x80\x98to be on the left hand; to be twisted, distorted\xe2\x80\x99 (CDG 149), Tna. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc3\xa4gam \xe2\x80\x98left\xe2\x80\x99 (TED 2616), Tgr. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc3\xa4gg\xc3\xa4m\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to be difficult\xe2\x80\x99 (WTS 648) as well as Arb. \xe1\xb8\x8d\xc7\xafm \xe2\x80\x98to be crooked, distorted (mouth),\xe2\x80\x99 \xe1\xb8\x8da\xc7\xafam- \xe2\x80\x98inclining towards one side of the face\xe2\x80\x99 (Lane 1770). (Kogan 2015:573)\n
