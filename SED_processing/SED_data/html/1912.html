\n \n elibbu\xe1\xb8\xabu - bladder (of a bird, sheep or fish)\n (CAD e 89, AHw 203)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

Variants: libbu\xe1\xb8\xabu, ilibbu\xe1\xb8\xabu, ellambu\xe1\xb8\xabu, ilbu\xe1\xb8\xabu, illabu\xe1\xb8\xabu, ellabu\xe1\xb8\xabu.\xc2\xa0Von Soden suggests that this is a Sum. loanword (see a detailed discussions in [Lieberman 222]). Part of the forms with prefixed \xcb\x80i-\xc2\xad (< *\xcb\x80a- influenced by \xc2\xad-i-\xc2\xad of the second syllable implying *\xcb\x80a-libbu\xe1\xb8\xab- \xc2\xad?).\xc2\xa0
