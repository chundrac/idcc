PS
\n
\n

*pa\xe1\xb8\xa5l- - penis\n (SED I No. 210; Kogan 2011: 219)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Semantically a complicated case. One of the derived meanings to be reconstructed as PS is \xe2\x80\x98domestic animal (used to fecundate female cattle)\xe2\x80\x99.\xc2\xa0There is also a verbal root more probably produced on the common Sem. level than in each individual language independently. The Afras. data, though scarce, and the typology of semantic evolution speak for \xe2\x80\x98thigh\xe2\x80\x99 (see Akk. pa\xe1\xb8\xaballu) as the primary meaning of this root developed into \xe2\x80\x98penis\xe2\x80\x99 (and further into \xe2\x80\x98domestic animal\xe2\x80\x99 and \xe2\x80\x98to be sexually aroused\xe2\x80\x99), and not vice versa.\xc2\xa0For analogy see\xc2\xa0*pa\xe1\xb8\xabi\xe1\xb8\x8f-\xc2\xa0- \xe2\x80\x98hip, thigh\xe2\x80\x99\xc2\xa0(No. 211).\xc2\xa0
