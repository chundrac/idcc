*sab\xcb\x80- - man\n (Kogan 2015:447)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n \xe2\x80\x9cThe etymology of Proto-EthS *sab\xcb\x80 is uncertain. One cannot exclude an ultimate connection with Sab. s1b\xcb\x80 \xe2\x80\x98to carry out an undertaking, a military campaign\xe2\x80\x99, s1b\xcb\x80t \xe2\x80\x98expedition, undertaking, journey\xe2\x80\x99 (SD 122), Qat. s1b\xcb\x80 \xe2\x80\x98to set out, go\xe2\x80\x99 (LIQ 157). This comparison would imply an original meaning \xe2\x80\x9cgang\xe2\x80\x9d, \xe2\x80\x9cmilitary or working commando.\xe2\x80\x9d Generalization of the ethnonym s1b\xcb\x80 \xe2\x80\x98Saba, Sabaeans\xe2\x80\x99 (cf. LLA 359 and Appleyard 1977:8) is also to be seriously considered.\xe2\x80\x9d\n
