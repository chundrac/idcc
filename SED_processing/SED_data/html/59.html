*\xe1\xb8\x8fV - this, that; the one who/which/that\n (CDG 629, Kogan 2009:71, Kogan 2015:35)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Etymological parallels to the *\xe1\xb8\x8fV/*\xcb\x80Vl(l)- system in Akkadian are not quite transparent. As argued by J. Huehnergard (2006:116\xe2\x80\x92118), *\xe1\xb8\x8fV is cognate with the Akkadian relative pronoun \xc5\xa1a, whose prototype *\xe1\xb9\xafu can be reliably established on the basis of the Sargonic orthography with \xc5\xa0V signs. An obvious problem faced by this comparison (otherwise quite appealing if only for distributional reasons) is the apparently unmotivated shift from *\xe1\xb9\xaf to *\xe1\xb8\x8f (or vice versa).
\r\nThese difficulties notwithstanding, it still seems easier to assume (with Hasselbach 2007) that the WS situation reflects the original PS picture, whereas Akkadian, here again, has innovated.\n

\n \n Soqotri\n \n

\n \n de - this\n (LS 62, 120, 127, 131, 229; CSOL I 522-523; CSOL II 439-440; Naumkin et al. 2015a:66)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

De is used in the phrase de di-\xe1\xb8\xa5a  \xe2\x80\x98this (near deixis)\xe2\x80\x99 and de di-bo\xe1\xb8\xb3 \xe2\x80\x98that (far deixis)\xe2\x80\x99, see CSOL I:522. See also \xe1\xb8\xa5a, bo\xe1\xb8\xb3. f. de\xc5\xa1, du. d\xc3\xadki, pl. \xc4\xbehe
