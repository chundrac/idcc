*\xcb\x81rb/*\xce\xb3rb - to come in, to enter\n (Kogan 2015: 333;409)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n

\n \n Samalian\n \n
\n
\n \n \xcb\x81rb - to enter\n (KAI 215:18)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n J. Tropper (1993a:126) reads w-h\xcb\x81rb \xcb\x80by mn dm\xc5\xa1\xe1\xb8\xb3 l-\xcb\x80\xc5\xa1w[r] in KAI 215:18 and translates \xe2\x80\x98Und er f\xc3\xbchrte meinen Vater von Damaskus hinein nach Assu[r].\xe2\x80\x99 If this reading is correct, Samalian would possess a verb \xcb\x81rb \xe2\x80\x98to come in, to enter,\xe2\x80\x99 cognate to Ugr. \xcb\x81rb and Akk. er\xc4\x93bu, but not attested in Aramaic. No certainty in this respect is possible, however: apart from the epigraphic difficulties, one may observe that neither Ugr. \xcb\x81rb nor Akk. er\xc4\x93bu are normally used with the prepositions meaning \xe2\x80\x9cfrom\xe2\x80\x9d (DUL 179\xe2\x80\x92180, CAD E 259\xe2\x80\x92273). Given the fact that Hbr. \xcb\x81br with min is, conversely, well attested (HALOT 780,\r\nmeaning 8), one may wonder whether the traditional reading h\xcb\x81br, still accepted in KAI5, is worth preserving (Kogan 2015:409).\n
