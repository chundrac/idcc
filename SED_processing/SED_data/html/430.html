Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*\xcb\x81amal- - field\n (Kogan 2015: 549)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n \xe2\x80\x9cProto-MSA *\xcb\x81amal- must be identical to Arb. \xcb\x81amal- \xe2\x80\x98work, labor\xe2\x80\x99 (and PCS *\xcb\x81amal-). In Classical Arabic this term does not seem to display any agricultural meaning which is, however, well attested in the Arabic dialects of Southern Arabia (Landberg 1901:663, GD 2330\xe2\x80\x922331, Behnstedt 867, Piamenta 341, Rhodokanakis\r\n41). In such conditions, it is hard to say whether we are faced with a (relatively\r\nearly?) Arabic loanword into individual MSA languages or rather with\r\na Proto-MSA term which influenced the local Arabic dialects. Admittedly, the\r\nphonetic shape of the Soqotri word (final stress, the \xe2\x80\x9csoft\xe2\x80\x9d l\xca\xbc) rather unambiguously\r\nspeaks in favor of the the first possibility.\xe2\x80\x9d (Kogan 2015:549)\n
