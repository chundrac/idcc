Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*\xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb8\xa5r - to brand\n (Kogan 2015:569\xe2\x80\x93570)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Leslau is likely correct to relate Proto-MSA *\xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb8\xa5r \xe2\x80\x98to brand\xe2\x80\x99 to Hbr. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc4\x81\xe1\xb8\xa5\xc5\x8dr \xe2\x80\x98reddish-gray\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 1019), Syr. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb8\xa5ar \xe2\x80\x98erubuit\xe2\x80\x99 (LSyr. 626), Arb. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb8\xa5r (XI) \xe2\x80\x98to dry up, to become yellow,\xe2\x80\x99 \xe1\xb9\xa3a\xe1\xb8\xa5\xc4\xabrat- \xe2\x80\x98milk into which heated stones are thrown\xe2\x80\x99 (Lane 1654), implying an original meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto burn,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cto be burnt\xe2\x80\x9d (cf. especially Ugr. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xe1\xb8\xa5rr \xe2\x80\x98to be burnt,\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x98to roast,\xe2\x80\x99 DUL 782). One might be tempted to relate to this root Tgr. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc3\xa4\xe1\xb8\xa5ar\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to take up and carry away coal and ashes\xe2\x80\x99, Tna. \xe1\xb9\xa3\xc3\xa4\xe1\xb8\xa5ar\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to stir a fire\xe2\x80\x99, but the general meaning of the Tigrinya verb seems rather to be \xe2\x80\x98to scoop up, to scrape up with the hands, to shovel\xe2\x80\x99 (Kogan 2015:569-570)\n
