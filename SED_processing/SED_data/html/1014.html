*gVb(V)b-, gVb(b)-an- - hump, hunchback\n (SED I No. 67; Kogan 2011: 230)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The root is likely related to *ganb\xc2\xad- \xe2\x80\x98side and back of torso\xe2\x80\x99 (SED I No. 85).\r\nThough traditionally identified with Sem. *gbb \xe2\x80\x98to be bent\xe2\x80\x99 ([CDG 177], [DRS\r\n94]), the present root is rather influenced by, than related to, the latter.\n

\n \n gibb\xc4\x93n - hunchbacked\n (HALOT 174)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n A hapax whose alternative interpretation is \xe2\x80\x98one having defective eyebrow\xe2\x80\x99; though in most Hebrew dictionaries this word is interpreted as \xe2\x80\x98humpbacked\xe2\x80\x99, the context of Lv 21: 20 rather suggests a defect in the eye area.\n
