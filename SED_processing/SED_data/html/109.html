*bay\xe1\xb9\xa3\xcc\x82-at- - egg\n (SED I No. 43, Kogan 2015:175)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Usually unseparated from Akk. pe\xe1\xb9\xa3\xc3\xbb and Arb. by\xe1\xb8\x8d \xe2\x80\x98to be white\xe2\x80\x99, which are likely to form a different root *pay\xe1\xb9\xa3\xcc\x82-\xc2\xad including also Gez. be\xe1\xb9\xa3\xc4\x81, b\xc9\x99\xe1\xb9\xa3\xc4\x81 \xe2\x80\x98yellow\xe2\x80\x99, by\xe1\xb9\xa3 \xe2\x80\x98to become white, yellow\xe2\x80\x99 [LGz. 116] (\xc2\xad\xe1\xb9\xa3 being a graphic variant of the expected *-\xe1\xb8\x8d?), Amh. b\xc9\x99\xc4\x8d\xcc\xa3a, bi\xc4\x8d\xcc\xa3a \xe2\x80\x98yellow\xe2\x80\x99 [K 950] and Tgr. b\xc3\xa4yy\xc3\xa4\xe1\xb9\xa3\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to be bright, brilliant\xe2\x80\x99 [LH 295].\r\nAccording to a widespread opinion, goes back to the color term *by\xc5\x9d\xcc\xa3 \xe2\x80\x98to be white, bright, yellow\xe2\x80\x99. If this etymology is correct, we are faced with a PCS semantic innovation, but the opposite way of derivation (\xe2\x80\x9cegg\xe2\x80\x9d > \xe2\x80\x9cwhite\xe2\x80\x9d) has also been suggested (F. Rosenthal apud CDG 116). In any case, this wide-spread opinion formulated by Leslau as \xc2\xabthe root by\xe1\xb9\xa3 \xe2\x8c\xa9erroneously for by\xe1\xb8\x8d. - A. M. /L. K.\xe2\x8c\xaa \xe2\x80\x98become white\xe2\x80\x99 is the origin of the noun \xe2\x80\x98egg\xe2\x80\x99 in ... various Semitic languages\xc2\xbb [LGz. 116] should probably be revised in the light of W. Chad. parallels: Geji mbuu\xc5\x9d\xc3\xac, Buli mbi\xc5\x9d, Tule mb\xc3\xb2\xc3\xb2\xc5\x9d\xc9\x99, Zaar bu\xc3\xb9\xc5\x9d \xe2\x80\x98egg\xe2\x80\x99 [Stolb. 1996 122] (reconstructed as *m-buy/\xcb\x80V\xc5\x9d\xc2\xad [ibid.] corresponding to Afras. *buya\xc4\x8d\xcc\xa3\xc2\xad after [OS 86]). \r\nIn HSED 86, PCS *bay\xc5\x9d-\xcc\xa3 at- has been compared to hypothetical West Chadic cognates, which would mean that the PCS term is a retention from PAA. Cf.\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n Arb. bay\xe1\xb8\x8f\xcc\xa3 \xe2\x80\x98oeuf de fourmi\xe2\x80\x99 \xe2\x80\x9cant egg\xe2\x80\x9d [Belot Arb.-Fr. 50] (not in [BK 1 185], where bay\xe1\xb8\x8f\xcc\xa3 means \xe2\x80\x98liqueur n\xc3\xa9cessaire \xc3\xa0 la g\xc3\xa9n\xc3\xa9ration; sperme\xe2\x80\x99). \r\nOne wonders whether a variant Sem. root *bay\xe1\xb9\xaf\xcc\xa3-\xc2\xad with *\xe1\xb9\xaf\xcc\xa3 vs. *\xe1\xb9\xa3\xcc\x82 can be reconstructed on the latter Arb. and MSA parallels.\n
