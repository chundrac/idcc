*\xe1\xb8\xa5km - to be wise\n (Kogan 2015: 82\xe2\x80\x9383)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The status of this isogloss as a PWS feature is undermined by several circumstances(...):\r\n
(1) The autochthonous vs. borrowed status of Akk. \xe1\xb8\xabak\xc4\x81mu \xe2\x80\x98to know, to understand\xe2\x80\x99 has been hotly debated by several generations\r\nof Assyriologists and Semitists (v. Kogan 2011:111 for a select bibliography) (...)\r\n(2) The Geez verb is attested only once in Pr 17:28. The semantic link between \xe2\x80\x98se cohibere, continere\xe2\x80\x99 (LLA 112) and \xe2\x80\x9cto be wise\xe2\x80\x9d is far from trivial, but fits well the sapiential context of \xe2\x80\x9crestraining one\xe2\x80\x99s lips in order to look wise.\xe2\x80\x9d All other representatives of *\xe1\xb8\xa5km in EthS are borrowed from Arabic (Leslau 1990:341)\r\n
(3) Most of the meanings associated with this root in MSA are almost certainly due to Arabic influence. This is, however, much less evident as far as the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto be old\xe2\x80\x9d is concerned: while also attested in Arabic (\xe1\xb8\xa5akam- \xe2\x80\x98a man advanced in age to the utmost degree,\xe2\x80\x99 Lane 617), it seems to be too marginal there to be considered a reliable source of borrowing.\r\n
(4) As reasonably argued by A. Jeffery (1938:111), the genuinely Arabic meanings of \xe1\xb8\xa5km are \xe2\x80\x9cmore in connection with the sense of govern,\xe2\x80\x9d whereas the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto be wise\xe2\x80\x9d is likely due to Hebrew and Aramaic influence.\n
