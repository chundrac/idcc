*\xe1\xb8\xa5urr- - free, noble ones\n (Kogan 2015: 211)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The origin of PCS *\xe1\xb8\xa5urr- \xe2\x80\x98free ones\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. Its relationship to Gez. \xe1\xb8\xa5ar\xc4\x81 is a complex issue. The meaning of the Geez term is \xe2\x80\x9carmy, troops,\xe2\x80\x9d and its nisbah-derivate \xe1\xb8\xa5ar\xc4\x81wi, too, is usually connected with the military sphere (\xe2\x80\x98soldier, captain,\xe2\x80\x99 CDG 240). However, in one late attestation quoted in LLA 85\xe2\x80\x9286 \xe1\xb8\xa5ar\xc4\x81wi denotes a freeborn person (as opposed to a slave). The same meaning is prominent in modern EthS. It lies at hand to suppose that the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cfree\xe2\x80\x9d in Geez is due to a late Arabic infiltration. Leslau (CDG 241)\r\nis probably correct to suspect that the meanings \xe2\x80\x9carmy\xe2\x80\x9d and \xe2\x80\x9cfreeborn, noble\xe2\x80\x9d\r\nare probably unconnected\r\n(Kogan 2015: 211)\n
