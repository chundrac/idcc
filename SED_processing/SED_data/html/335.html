\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n ba\xcb\x80latu - lady\n (CAD B 45, AHw. 98)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Hapax.\r\nA WS feminine form of *ba\xcb\x81l - \xe2\x80\x9clord\xe2\x80\x9d.\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n
\n
\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n ba\xcb\x80l\xc4\xabyu - (a synonym for lord, master)\n (CAD B 63)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n As a synonym of b\xc4\x93lu \xe2\x80\x9clord\xe2\x80\x9d, the present form must go back to WS *ba\xcb\x81l -. Following SAD 1 7, one has to assume that a nisbah suffix was attached to *ba\xcb\x81l -, even if this is rather unusual.\n
