\n \n Ugaritic\n \n
\n
\n \n p\xcb\x80it, p\xcb\x80at - temple; boundary, border, side, fringe\n (DUL 649, Huehnergard 1991:695, EDA I 73)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n The meaning of Ugr. p\xcb\x80it is debatable. Dietrich; Loretz 1990, 145f. opt for \xe2\x80\x9cforehead\xe2\x80\x9d, but since 1.103+:11 involves a paired body part ({l p\xe1\xbb\x89t \xc5\xa1m\xe1\xba\xa3l} \xe2\x80\x9con his left p\xe1\xbb\x89t\xe2\x80\x9d), Pardee\xe2\x80\x99s arguments in favour of the translation \xe2\x80\x9ctemple\xe2\x80\x9d (Pardee 2000, 560f.) are cogent. However, in 1.17 ii 9 ({w \xca\x95l y\xe1\xb9\xa3hl p\xe1\xbb\x89t}) the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cforehead\xe2\x80\x9d would fit much better than \xe2\x80\x9ctemple\xe2\x80\x9d (cf. Pardee\xe2\x80\x99s rather strained \xe2\x80\x9cabove, he shines in (his) temples\xe2\x80\x9d, paraphrased as \xe2\x80\x9chis countenance glowed\xe2\x80\x9d in the literary translation, Pardee 1997c, 345).\n

\n \n Hebrew\n \n

\n \n p\xc5\x8dt - brow, forehead\n (HALOT 983)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

pl.\xc2\xa0p\xc5\x8dt\xc5\x8dt.\xc2\xa0Note the form with pronominal suffx pot-h\xc4\x93n \xe2\x80\x98their (fem.) p\xc5\x8dt\xe2\x80\x99 in Is 3:17, which definitely implies the protoform *put-\xc2\xad. The present meaning is attested in the Bible in two ambiguous contexts only: in 1K 7:50 denoting part of the door, translated as \xe2\x80\x98front side, facade\xe2\x80\x99 in [HALOT 983] and alternatively interpreted as \xe2\x80\x98socket of the door pin\xe2\x80\x99 in [Jastrow 1149]; and in Is 3:17 in the phrase\xc2\xa0\xc2\xabThe Lord will make scabby the skull (\xe1\xb8\xb3od\xe1\xb8\xb3\xc5\x8dd) of the daughters of Zion and Yhwh will lay bare their p\xc5\x8dt\xc2\xbb (on interpreting this term as \xe2\x80\x98female pudenda\xe2\x80\x99 see *pV\xcb\x80w/y(-at)-\xc2\xa0- genital organ, No. 206).

\n \n Jewish Babylonian Aramaic\n \n
\n
\n \n \xcb\x80\xc4\x83p\xc5\xabt\xc4\x81 - forehead\n (DJBA 154)\n \n
