Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*ma-lsay- - rain\n (Kogan 2015:543)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n \xe2\x80\x9cOne is tempted to connect Proto-MSA *ma-lsay- \xe2\x80\x98rain\xe2\x80\x99 with Akk. nal\xc5\xa1u \xe2\x80\x98dew\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD N1 202, AHw. 724). The unusual sequence of two sonorants in the Akkadian word makes feasible the secondary origin of of n-, whereas the correspondence of Akk. (and PS) \xc5\xa1 to MSA s has good precedents elsewhere in the MSA vocabulary. This comparison is, however, quite tentative in any case.\xe2\x80\x9d\n
