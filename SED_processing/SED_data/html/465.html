\n

*hVlV\xcb\x81- - shadow\n (Kogan 2015:556)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The origin of Proto-MSA *hVlV\xcb\x81- \xe2\x80\x98shadow\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. As there is no feasible parallel with *h-, one may be inclined to agree with the authors of DRS 418 who, following Leslau, surmise that the first consonant of the protoform is *\xc5\xa1, comparing it to Arb. sil\xcb\x81- \xe2\x80\x98a like, a fellow\xe2\x80\x99. The etomology is contractied by the presence of h in Jibbali, but, as rightly acknowledged in DRS, the Jibbali lexeme seems to be specifically poetic and, therefore, probably Mehrizing (Kogan 2015:556)\n
