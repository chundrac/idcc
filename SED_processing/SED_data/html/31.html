*bi/ark- - knee\n (SED I No. 39)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Cf. *rVk(u)b(\xc2\xadat)\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99 (No. 232) and discussion in [GD 163], where separation of these two roots is also favored.\n

\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n birku, burku - knee, lap\n (CAD B 255, AHw. 129)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n -\xc2\xadu-\xc2\xad in Akk. (alongside with \xc2\xad-i-\xc2\xad) and Arm. (Syr. and Mnd.) is likely a secondary development under the influence of b-\xc2\xad.\r\nFor the meaning \xe2\x80\x98lap\xe2\x80\x99, see *barak-\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98chest, thorax\xe2\x80\x99. See discussion in [Holma 1911 95] where the author tends to treat birku \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99 and birku \xe2\x80\x98genitals\xe2\x80\x99 as the same word, quoting, at the same time, Arb. rakab-\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98pubis\xe2\x80\x99 as a possible cognate to the latter.\n

\n \n Arabic\n \n
\n
\n \n b\xc4\x81rikat-\xc2\xad - to kneel\n (Lane 193, BK 1 116)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Formed after the active participle pattern; strangely enough, usually not quoted in comparative studies (see [DRS], [LGz]). Cf. also brk \xe2\x80\x98s\xe2\x80\x99agenouiller\xe2\x80\x99 [BK 1 116]. As for bark\xc2\xad-, birk\xc2\xad- \xe2\x80\x98genou\xe2\x80\x99 quoted in [GD 153], it is not clear whether it is Da\xe1\xb9\xaf. or Mhr.\n
