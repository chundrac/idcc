*\xe1\xb9\xadayr- - bird\n (SED II No. 235)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Attested as a faunal term in Arm. and Arb. only (unless Tna. \xc4\x8d\xcc\xa3\xc9\x99ru \xe2\x80\x98small bird\xe2\x80\x99 in TED 2503 and Msq. \xc4\x8d\xcc\xa3\xc9\x99rri, Gog. \xc4\x8d\xcc\xa3\xc9\x99rriy\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98kind of bird\xe2\x80\x99 in EDG 187 are related, which is difficult to prove). To be nevertheless reconstructed as a PS animal name in view of clearly related forms connected with divination from birds whose attestation is not confined to Arm. and Arb. See further Wol. \xe1\xb9\xadur, Sel. \xe1\xb9\xad\xc5\xabr \xe2\x80\x98good fortune, luck\xe2\x80\x99 (EDG 630), Cha. E\xc5\xbea. Muh. Msq. Gog. Sod. \xe1\xb9\xadur, Enn. End. Gye. \xe1\xb9\xad\xc5\xabr id. (ibid.), probably to be kept apart from Amh. \xe1\xb9\xadur \xe2\x80\x98punishment which befalls a wrongdoer, arrogant behaviour, insult\xe2\x80\x99 (contra Leslau) but rather connected with the present root (for -u- cf. Arb. \xe1\xb9\xad\xc5\xabrat- \xe2\x80\x98bad augury\xe2\x80\x99, BK 2 129).\n
