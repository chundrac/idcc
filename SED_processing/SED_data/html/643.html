In the Vienna corpus two lexemes clearly represent the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cbird\xe2\x80\x9d: n\xc3\xb3yh\xc9\x99r and \xc9\x99\xe1\xb9\xa3f\xc3\xa9ro. The former is slightly less common than the later, and these terms seems to be synonymous. In the dialect of the Da\xe2\x80\x99rho tribe, however, only n\xc3\xb3yh\xc9\x99r is used as a general term for \xe2\x80\x9cbird\xe2\x80\x9d, while \xc9\x99\xe1\xb9\xa3f\xc3\xa9ro is said to designate a concrete bird species (\xe2\x80\x9dsparrow\xe2\x80\x9d). There is also a possibility that n\xc3\xb3yh\xc9\x99r and \xc9\x99\xe1\xb9\xa3f\xc3\xa9ro are used to as generic terms for big and small birds respectively.
