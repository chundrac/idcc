Areal reconstruction
\n
\n

*kms - to buckle, to bend\n (Kogan 2015:336)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

Akk.-Ugr. *kms is vaguely reminiscent of Akkadian and WS anatomic terms denoting \xe2\x80\x9carticulation,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cjoint\xe2\x80\x9d (Akk. kim\xe1\xb9\xa3u, Hbr. \xe1\xb8\xb3\xc9\x99m\xc4\x81\xe1\xb9\xa3\xc4\xabm, see SED I No. 172), but no phonologically exact cognate has been detected so far.

\n
\n \n Ugaritic\n \n
\n
\n \n kms - to buckle, to bend\n (DUL 446, Watson 2007: 88)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

As reasonably surmised in HALOT 1109, it is to this root that the Ugaritic verb \xe1\xb8\xb3m\xe1\xb9\xa3 \xe2\x80\x98to curl up, to bend\xe2\x80\x99 likely belongs. This comparison invalidates Watson\xe2\x80\x99s conclusion that Ugr. \xe1\xb8\xb3m\xe1\xb9\xa3 is borrowed from Akkadian (Watson 2007:101).
