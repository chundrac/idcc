\n \n Hebrew\n \n
\n
\n \n da\xcb\x81at - sweat\n (HALOT 229)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n The specific reflexes of *\xe1\xb8\x8f in Hbr. (regular z\xc2\xad and irregular d\xc2\xad) are noteworthy. An alternative Arb. parallel fits both phonetically and semantically: zu\xce\xb3\xce\xb3- \xe2\x80\x98odeur f\xc3\xa9tide des\r\naisselle, surtout chez n\xc3\xa8gres\xe2\x80\x99 [BK 1 993]. If compared to all Sem. forms with z , it can yield a protoform *zu/i\xce\xb3(\xce\xb3)-\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98sweat\xe2\x80\x99. In this case, Hbr. and Eth. forms with z are to be related to the Sem. latter root and to *zu/i\xce\xb3(\xce\xb3)-\xc2\xad (the Hbr. forms in d still would have to be recognized as continuing Sem. forms in *d-\xc2\xad or as early borrowings from Arm.)\n
