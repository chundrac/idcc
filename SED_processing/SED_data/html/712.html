*gVrgVr-at- - throat\n (SED I No. 102; Kogan 2011: 216)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n A descriptive nominal root. In Arb. and MSA, only derived verbs are preserved: Arb. \xc7\xafr\xc7\xafr \xe2\x80\x98avaler, boire en faisant entendre un glouglou\xe2\x80\x99 (to swallow; to drink making a gurgling sound) [BK 1 274]; Jib. eg\xc9\x9brg\xc3\xa9r \xe2\x80\x98to gurgle\xe2\x80\x99 [JL 78]\n

\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n si(g)gaggur\xc4\xabtu, singang/ur\xc4\xabtu - throat\n (AHw. 1039)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Note that in [CAD s 284] it is translated as \xe2\x80\x98part of the foot\xe2\x80\x99 with the following comment: \xe2\x80\x9cThe restoration ZI.IN.GI in Hh. and Hg. assumes that the Akk. word incorporates the Sum. element\xe2\x80\x9d (ZI.IN.GI \xe2\x80\x98Kn\xc3\xb6chel\xe2\x80\x99 (\xe2\x80\x99ankle\xe2\x80\x99) [IK 1187], apud [AHw. 488]). The reading of this word as gaggur\xc4\xabtu in [CAD g 9] is rejected in [CAD s 284].\n
