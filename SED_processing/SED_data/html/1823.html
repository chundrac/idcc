\n \n fqd - to miss, to lose, not to find; V. visiter, examiner une chose, la consid\xc3\xa9rer attentivement, y faire attention, en prendre soin (to visit, examine something, to consider it carefully, to pay attention to it, to take care of it)\n (Lane 2424, Dozy II 271)\n \n

\n\n
\n The meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto muster troops\xe2\x80\x9d, not registered for fqd in the standard Arabic dictionaries, clearly appears in the Koran (27:20):\r\nwa-tafaqqada \xe1\xb9\xad-\xe1\xb9\xadayra fa-q\xc4\x81la m\xc4\x81 liya l\xc4\x81 \xca\x94ar\xc4\x81 l-hudhuda \xe2\x80\x9cAnd he [Solomon] mustered the birds [called \xc7\xa7un\xc5\xabduhu \xe2\x80\x9chis troops\xe2\x80\x9d above in\r\nverse 17] and said: \xe2\x80\x9cHow is it that I do not see the hoopoe?\xe2\x80\x9d (EDA I 179)\n
