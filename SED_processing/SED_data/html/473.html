*\xe1\xb8\xab\xc5\xabr-, *\xe1\xb8\xabarr-Vn- - a little\n (Kogan 2015:559)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The origin of Proto-MSA *\xe1\xb8\xab\xc5\xb1r-, *\xe1\xb8\xabarr-Vn- \xe2\x80\x98a little\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain, although A. Jahn\xe2\x80\x99s comparison (1902:199) with Arb. \xe1\xb8\xabwr \xe2\x80\x98to be weak, feeble\xe2\x80\x99 is not unreasonable, see further Gez. \xe1\xb8\xab\xc9\x99wwur \xe2\x80\x98weak, invalid\xe2\x80\x99, Tgr. \xe1\xb8\xa5aw\xc3\xa4r\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98perdre la parole (de faiblesse)\xe2\x80\x99, compared to Mhr. \xe1\xb8\xabawr in CDG 269. Semantically more attractive is M. Bittner\xe2\x80\x99s equation (1915a:40-41) with Arb. \xe1\xb8\xa5wr \xe2\x80\x98to decrease, to be defective or deficient\xe2\x80\x99 (Lane 665), but one is reluctant to accept it because of the phonological difference (Kogan 2015:559)\n
