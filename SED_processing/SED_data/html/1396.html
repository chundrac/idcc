\n
\n \n *ba\xe1\xb9\xadn- - belly\n (SED I No. 42; Kogan 2011: 217)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n In Arm presumably ousted in its basic meaning by the reflexes of PS *kari\xc5\x9d- \xe2\x80\x98stomach\xe2\x80\x99. Its existence in Proto-Aramaic can nevertheless be reliably deduced from the widespread verbal root *b\xe1\xb9\xadn \xe2\x80\x98to be pregnant\xe2\x80\x99\r\nNote parallel forms with \xc2\xad-i\xc2\xad- in Hbr. and Arm. Cf. such probably derived terms as Can.: Amn. b\xe1\xb9\xadn, designation of an architectural element [HJ 151], and Pho. b\xe1\xb9\xadn \xe2\x80\x98embossement(?)\xe2\x80\x99 [T 45].\r\nNote meaning shifts in part of Arm.\n
