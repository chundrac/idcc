\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n b\xc3\xa2\xcb\x80u - to walk along, go through (a street, a road, a square, a gate, etc.), to pass over (a region)\n (CAD B 178, AHw. 116)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n OA, Mari, EA, rare in SB.\n
\n
\n\n
\n

\n
\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n b\xc3\xa2\xcb\x80u - to come\n (CAD: s.v. b\xc3\xa2\xcb\x80u, AHw. 117, CDA 41)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n The verb, etymologically identical with b\xc3\xa2\xcb\x80u \xe2\x80\x9cto go along\xe2\x80\x9d, is, however, attested in Mari with a different thematic vowel (\xc5\xab vs. \xc4\x81) and displays a different meaning (\xe2\x80\x9cto come\xe2\x80\x9d vs. \xe2\x80\x9cto pass, to go along\xe2\x80\x9d). The cumulative evidence of the vowel, the meaning and the dialectal distribution makes it almost certain that these examples should be interpreted as attestations of the WS root *bw\xcb\x80 \xe2\x80\x9cto come\xe2\x80\x9d.\n
