*-\xc5\xa1\xc5\xab\xcc\x86 - his; him (genitive and accusative pronominal suffix, 3 masc. sg.)\n (Kogan 2009:69)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The shift *\xc5\xa1- > h is distributed between languages the same way as in the realm of independent pronouns of the 3rd person.\n

\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n -\xc5\xa1u - his; him (genitive and accusative pronominal suffix, 3 masc. sg.)\n (GAG \xc2\xa7 42 g)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Sargonic orthography distinguishes between genitive -SU and accusative SU\xe2\x82\x84.\n
\n
\n\n
\n

\n \n Geez\n \n
\n
\n \n -o, -hu - him (accusative pronominal suffix, 3 masc. sg.)\n (Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:101)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n -hu is the allomorph used with verbal forms ending in *-\xc4\x81 (the etymological guttural is restored). See Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:105.\n
\n
\n\n
\n

\n \n Geez\n \n
\n
\n \n -u/-o, -hu - his (genitive pronominal suffix, 3 masc. sg.)\n (CDG 213, Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:101)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n The form of the possessive suffix with nouns in nominative case is -u, while the allomorph used with nouns in accusative is -o. -hu is the allomorph used with forms ending in a vowel. See Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:103, 105.\n
