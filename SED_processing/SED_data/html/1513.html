*\xcb\x80antum\xc5\xab\xcc\x86 - you (masc. pl.)\n (Kogan 2009:67)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n In individual languages there are different ways of opposing the masculine form of the 2 pl. pronoun to the feminine one. In Akk. the two forms are opposed through the vocalic contrast in the second and third syllables (masc. -u-...-u vs. fem. -i-...-a) with no consonantal contrast (-n- in both genders). In Heb., Arb., Gez., Hrs., Jib. the gender opposition is provided by the third consonant (masc. -m- vs. fem. -n-) alongside with some vocalic differences. The mere consonantal opposition seems to be present only in Mehri. See Kogan 2009:67.\n
