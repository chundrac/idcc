*dhm - to be dark\n (Fronzaroli 1965a: 138\xe2\x80\x93\x02139, 145, 149; Kogan 2011: 196; EDA II)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

One cannot exclude that Hbr. nidh\xc4\x81m \xe2\x80\x9castounded, confused\xe2\x80\x9d (HALOT 214) belongs to this root with a metaphoric shift of meaning (< \xe2\x80\x9cdark, gloomy\xe2\x80\x9d, \xd1\x81f. Russian \xd0\xbc\xd1\x80\xd0\xb0\xd1\x87\xd0\xbd\xd1\x8b\xd0\xb9). Admittedly, the context of the only passage where it is attested (Jr 14:9) rather suggests the meaning \xe2\x80\x9chelpless\xe2\x80\x9d.
