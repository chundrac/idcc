Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*lkd - to patch leather\n (Kogan 2015:563)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Leslau is probably correct to connect Proto-MSA *lkd \xe2\x80\x98to patch leather\xe2\x80\x99 with Hbr. lkd \xe2\x80\x98to catch animals by trapping\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 530) and Arb. lkd \xe2\x80\x98to cleave, to stick\xe2\x80\x99 (Lane 2671), all going back to the presumably original meanings \xe2\x80\x9cto tie,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cto bind,\xe2\x80\x9d \xe2\x80\x9cto attach.\xe2\x80\x9d1466 Sab. mlkd \xe2\x80\x98cistern\xe2\x80\x99 (SD 82) likely belongs to the same root (with Biella 259: \xe2\x80\x9csomething which traps the water brought by the canal\xe2\x80\x9d). (Kogan 2015:563)\n
