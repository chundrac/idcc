*\xcb\x80ad\xc4\x81n- - lord\n (Kogan 2015: 279)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

likely represents an extension of a Lallwort for \xe2\x80\x9cfather,\xe2\x80\x9d still preserved in Ugaritic as \xcb\x80ad
\r\nhard to decide whether the element *\xcb\x80ad- is an independent Canaanite formation or an early loanword from a non-Semitic source, cf. Hit. atta- (Friedrich 38), Hur. attai (Laroche 63), Sum. ad(-da) (PSD A\xe2\x82\x83 9, with several orthographic variants). At any rate, both the extension in -\xc4\x81n- and the (presumably derived) meaning \xe2\x80\x9clord\xe2\x80\x9d must be regarded as PC innovations (Garbini 1984:94). Although one cannot exclude that *-\xc4\x81n- is an adaptation of the Hurrian determinate form attani (cf. Sanmart\xc3\xadn 1977:271)
