*\xe1\xb8\xa5ll - to be profane\n (Kogan 2015: 189)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The origin of PCS *\xe1\xb8\xa5ll \xe2\x80\x98to be profane\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. An ultimate relationship with Akk. el\xc4\x93lu \xe2\x80\x98to become pure\xe2\x80\x99 has been almost universally accepted (v. references in DRS 870\xe2\x80\x92872), but the enantiosemantic correspondence \xe2\x80\x9cto be pure\xe2\x80\x9d : \xe2\x80\x9cto be profane\xe2\x80\x9d is, in fact, far from evident.\n
