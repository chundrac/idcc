*\xc5\xa1\xc5\xab\xcc\x86/\xc5\xa1\xc5\xab\xcc\x86\xcb\x80/\xc5\xa1\xc5\xab\xcc\x86wa - he\n (Kogan 2009:66)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Forms with the initial sibilant, whose primary nature cannot be doubted in view of the data of non-Semitic Afroasiatic languages, are found in Akk., ESA (except for Sabaic) and, to a greater or lesser extent, in MSA. In Ug., Heb., Arm., Arb. and Sab. *\xc5\xa1- shifts to h- (Kogan 2009:66).\n

\n \n Geez\n \n
\n
\n \n w\xc9\x99\xcb\x80\xc9\x99tu - he\n (CDG 602)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n No convincing explanation of the loss of the initial *h has been suggested. The origin of the extension *-t\xc5\xab is not entirely certain. It may go back to the PS pronominal marker of the oblique case (see Akk. \xc5\xa1u\xc4\x81ti, Ug. hwt, Sab. hwt). However, it seems more probable to interpret it as the deictic element attested in other EthS pronominal forms (see, for example, Geez z\xc9\x99ntu). See Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:97\xe2\x80\x9398.\n

\n \n Tigrinya\n \n
\n
\n \n \xcb\x80\xc9\x99tu - that (masc. sg. demonstrative pronoun)\n (TED 1467)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Obviously related to Tigre h\xc9\x99tu, but preserved only with the function of the remote deixis pronoun. The origin of the extension *-t\xc5\xab is not entirely certain. It may go back to the PS pronominal marker of the oblique case (see Akk. \xc5\xa1u\xc4\x81ti, Ug. hwt, Sab. hwt). However, it seems more probable to interpret it as the deictic element attested in other EthS pronominal forms (see, for example, Geez z\xc9\x99ntu). See Bulakh\xe2\x80\x93Kogan 2013b:97\xe2\x80\x9398.\n
