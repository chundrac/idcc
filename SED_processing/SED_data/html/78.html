\n \n Soqotri\n \n

\n \n \xe1\xb8\xb3a\xc4\xbe\xc3\xadfo\xca\xb0 - bark\n (LS 376; CSOL I 588; CSOL II 512)\n \n

\n

\n

Within the Soqotri language, it is only \xe1\xb8\xb3al\xe2\x80\x99\xc3\xadfo\xca\xb0 that is strictly applied to the bark of trees, as in \xca\x95ag \xe1\xb8\xb3\xc3\xb6\xcc\x81\xe1\xb8\xb7of \xe1\xb8\xb3al\xe2\x80\x99\xc3\xadfo men \xc5\xa1\xc3\xa9rhom \xe2\x80\x98a man removed the bark from the tree\xe2\x80\x99. The word \xe1\xb8\xb3ar\xc3\xad\xc5\xa1o\xca\xb0 has a broader meaning, such as \xe2\x80\x9chusk\xe2\x80\x9d or \xe2\x80\x9cshell\xe2\x80\x9d. (Kogan 2015:486)
