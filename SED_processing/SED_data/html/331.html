*\xe1\xb8\xb3Vdm - in front of, before\n (Arakelova 2001:46, Kogan 2015:395)\n

\n\n
\n

\n \n Arabic\n \n
\n
\n \n qudd\xc4\x81m- - in front of\n (LA 12 42)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

There are good reasons to suspect that Arb. qudd\xc4\x81m- \xe2\x80\x98in front (of)\xe2\x80\x99 is due to Aramaic influence. While it is true that qudd\xc4\x81m- as naq\xc4\xab\xe1\xb8\x8du war\xc4\x81\xcb\x80in is mentioned in LA 12 42, one will not lose sight of the fact that both examples of the prepositional use quoted in Fr. III 410 are taken from an Arabic translation of Gospels. One wonders to what extent the broad presence of qudd\xc4\x81m in (pre-)modern Arabic dialects (Dozy II 324, 689, BH 689, Behnstedt 980) can be accounted for by Aramaic influence.
