*dawr-/*dawar- - period, cycle\n (DRS 240, Marassini 1971: 48, Fronzaroli 1965: 143, 148, EDA II)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

The \xe2\x80\x93 apparently iconic (descriptive) \xe2\x80\x93 reduplication *d\xc4\x81r-\xc2\xa0d\xc4\x81r-\xc2\xa0(or *d\xc4\x81r-\xc2\xa0dawr-), attested in both Akkadian and early NWS (Ugaritic, Hebrew, Aramaic), may go back to PS.

\n \n PS\n \n
\n
\n \n *dwr - to go around, to turn\n (EDA II)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

In various Semitic languages, PS *dwr \xe2\x80\x98to go round\xe2\x80\x99 acquired the meaning \xe2\x80\x98to stay permanently in one place, to dwell\xe2\x80\x99. A variant root *dhr is attested in some WS languages.

\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n d\xc4\x81ru - long time, eternity\n (AHw. 164, CAD D 107)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

See also d\xc5\xabru \xe2\x80\x98eternity; permanent position\xe2\x80\x99 (AHw. 178, CAD D 197),\xc2\xa0d\xc5\xabri\xc5\xa1 \xe2\x80\x98forever\xe2\x80\x99 (AHw. 178), d\xc4\x81ri\xc5\xa1 \xe2\x80\x98forever\xe2\x80\x99 (AHw. 163, CAD D 113),\xc2\xa0d\xc4\x81ri\xc5\xa1 \xe2\x80\x98for eternity\xe2\x80\x99 (AHw. 164, CAD D 114), d\xc4\x81r\xc4\x81nu \xe2\x80\x98eternal\xe2\x80\x99/\xe2\x80\x99eternal sacrifices\xe2\x80\x99 (AYw. 163, CAD D 109), d\xc4\x81r\xc4\xab\xcb\x80u \xe2\x80\x98(ever)lasting, eternal\xe2\x80\x99 (AHw. 164, CAD D 115),\xc2\xa0d\xc4\x81r\xc4\xab\xcb\x80a \xe2\x80\x98eternally\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD D 112), d\xc4\x81r\xc4\xabtu (AHw. 164, CAD D 114), d\xc4\x81r\xc5\xabtu (AHw. 164, CAD D 118), d\xc4\x81r\xc5\xabta\xc5\xa1\xc2\xa0(AHw. 1550, CAD D 118).\xc2\xa0This derivation, unquestionable in terms of the underlying consonantal root and the semantic development, is not without problems as far as derivational morphology is concerned, as the expected prototype\xc2\xa0*dawr-\xc2\xa0(Marrassini 1971: 48, Fronzaroli 1965: 143, 148) would yield\xc2\xa0d\xc5\xabru\xc2\xa0in Akkadian, actually well attested with the same meaning (see above).\xc2\xa0No ready solution for this problem\xc2\xa0is at hand.\xc2\xa0Shall one postulate a bivocalic pattern\xc2\xa0*dawar-\xc2\xa0contracted into *d\xc4\x81r-\xc2\xa0in the prehistory of Akkadian? Derivation from the by-form *dahr- is not to be ruled out completely, but does not seem likely.
