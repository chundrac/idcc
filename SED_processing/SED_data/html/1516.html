Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*sar - behind\n (Kogan 2015:569)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The origin of Proto-MSA *sar \xe2\x80\x98behind\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. Contra W. Leslau and M. Bittner (1914:15), any connection with PS *\xcb\x80a\xe1\xb9\xafar- \xe2\x80\x98trace\xe2\x80\x99 can be safely excluded for phonological reasons. Quite far-fetched is the comparison between the MSA terms and Arb. sar\xc4\xabr- \xe2\x80\x98the part where the head rests upon the neck\xe2\x80\x99 (Lane 1339), sar\xc4\x81t- \xe2\x80\x98back\xe2\x80\x99 (ibid. 1353), Amh. s\xc3\xa4r\xc3\xa4s\xc3\xa4r \xe2\x80\x98vertebra, spinal cord\xe2\x80\x99 (AED 487) suggested in SED I No. 253. Shall one rather compare Hbr. swr \xe2\x80\x98to turn aside\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 748), assuming a semantic development from the more original meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto turn back\xe2\x80\x9d? (Kogan 2015:569)\n
