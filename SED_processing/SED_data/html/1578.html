\n \n \xe1\xb9\xa3e\xe1\xb8\xab\xc4\x93ru - to become small, few, to be young\n (CAD \xe1\xb9\xa3 120)\n \n
\n
\n

\n \n Ugaritic\n \n
\n
\n \n s\xce\xb3r, s\xcc\x80\xce\xb3r - servant\n (DUL 755)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n < Akk. \xe1\xb9\xa3u\xe1\xb8\xab\xc4\x81ru \r\n PS *\xe1\xb9\xa3\xce\xb3r \xe2\x80\x98(to be) small\xe2\x80\x99 is well attested in Ugaritic in its original form. But it remains\r\nunclear why Akk. \xe1\xb8\xab and \xe1\xb9\xa3 should have shifted to \xce\xb3 and s (s\xcc\x80) in Ugaritic. The former correspondence could probably be explained by the speaker\xe2\x80\x99s awareness of the etymological relationship between \xe1\xb9\xa3u\xe1\xb8\xab\xc4\x81ru and \xe1\xb9\xa3\xce\xb3r (an etymologically motivated contamination, as in Biblical Aramaic h\xc4\x83l\xc4\x81k \xe2\x80\x98tribute, tax\xe2\x80\x99 < Akkadian ilku). The latter can only be accounted for by some sort of phonetic difference between Akkadian \xe1\xb9\xa3 and Ugaritic \xe1\xb9\xa3 (affricate vs. non-affricate or glottalized vs. backed?).\r\n (Kogan 2015: 353)\n
