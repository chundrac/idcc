*\xce\xb3rb - to know\n (Kogan 2015:542)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n \xe2\x80\x9cThe origin of Proto-MSA *\xce\xb3rb \xe2\x80\x98to know\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. The only immediate parallel is Sab. \xce\xb3rb \xe2\x80\x98to know, to be aware of, recognize\xe2\x80\x99, but one may wonder whether this sparsely attested verb is autochthonous in Sabaic. Comparison with Arb. \xcb\x81rf \xe2\x80\x98to know\xe2\x80\x99 accepted in LS and elsewhere implies two phonological irregularities and can hardly be appealing. Shall one tentatively\r\nsuspect an ultimate connection with PS *\xcb\x81rb/*\xce\xb3rb \xe2\x80\x98to come, to enter\xe2\x80\x99 (\xe2\x80\x9cto come across\xe2\x80\x9d > \xe2\x80\x9cto learn\xe2\x80\x9d)? For a recent attempt to connect Proto-MSA *\xce\xb3rb \xe2\x80\x98to know\xe2\x80\x99\r\nwith Arb. \xce\xb3ar\xc4\xabb- \xe2\x80\x98strange, unusual\xe2\x80\x99 and semantically similar lexemes within and outside Arabic v. Bulakh 2013b.\xe2\x80\x9d (Kogan 2015:542)\n
