*barak- - chest, thorax\n (SED I No. 38)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Rather to be separated from *bi/ark- \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99 (SED I No. 39).\r\nNote Akk. \xe2\x80\x98lap\xe2\x80\x99 in birku, burku [CAD b 255] given as a second meaning; this rather developed from the main meaning \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99 (see *bi/ark\xc2\xad- \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99), though it may also ensue from Sem. *barak-\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98chest\xe2\x80\x99.\n

\n \n Arabic\n \n
\n
\n \n bark- - skin of the belly of the camel and of the portion of the breast next to it; male breast\n (BK 1 116, Lane 193)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n The additional meaning related to kneeling appears to be due to contamination with *bi/ark\xc2\xad \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99.\n
\n

\n \n Amharic\n \n
\n
\n \n b\xc9\x99rakk\xca\xb7a - shoulder blade, bone of a cow\xe2\x80\x99s foreleg\n (AED 886)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n With labialization of -kk- under the influence of b-?\r\nWhile the first meaning rather relates to \xe2\x80\x98chest\xe2\x80\x99, the second may continue *bi/ark- \xe2\x80\x98knee\xe2\x80\x99 as well.\n
