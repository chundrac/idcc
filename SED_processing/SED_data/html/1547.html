Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*\xe1\xb9\xaf\xe1\xb8\xa5r - to wound\n (Kogan 2015:575)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n

In a personal communication, M. Krebernik suggests an interesting possibility of comparing proto-MSA *\xe1\xb9\xaf\xe1\xb8\xa5r \xe2\x80\x98to wound\xe2\x80\x99 with Sargonic Akkadian \xc5\xa1a\xcb\x80\xc4\x81ru (i\xc5\xa1\xe2\x82\x81\xe2\x82\x81-ar) \xe2\x80\x98to conquer, to win, to destroy\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD \xc5\xa0\xe2\x82\x81 2). This etymology provides a far better balance of semantic and phonological/orthographic features than the more traditional comparisons with either PS *\xe1\xb9\xaf\xce\xb3r \xe2\x80\x98to break\xe2\x80\x99 or Arabic \xe1\xb9\xaf\xcb\x80r \xe2\x80\x98to take blood-revenge.\xe2\x80\x99 (Kogan 2015:575, fn. 1493)
