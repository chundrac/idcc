*\xce\xb3ill-at- - mist\n (Kogan 2015:555)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n \xe2\x80\x9cThe origin of Proto-MSA *\xce\xb3ill-at- \xe2\x80\x98mist\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. Comparison with Gez. \xcb\x81awlo \xe2\x80\x98tempest, whirlwind, storm\xe2\x80\x99, Syr. \xcb\x81al\xcb\x81\xc4\x81l\xc4\x81 \xe2\x80\x98procella\xe2\x80\x99 seems attractive, whereas Leslau\xe2\x80\x99s identification with Arb. ya\xcb\x81l\xc5\xabl- \xe2\x80\x98clouds disposed one above another\xe2\x80\x99 is difficult because of the phonological irregularity. Furthermore, one cannot exclude that the Arabic noun is secondarily derived from the verbal root \xcb\x81ll with the general meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto repeat, to do a second time.\xe2\x80\x9d Also noteworthy are Arb. \xce\xb3alal- \xe2\x80\x98water having no current, only appearing a little upon the surface of the earth, disappearing at one time and appearing at another\xe2\x80\x99 and \xce\xb3ayl- \xe2\x80\x98water running upon the surface of the earth\xe2\x80\x99.\xe2\x80\x9d (Kogan 2015:555)\n
