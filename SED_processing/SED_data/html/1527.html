Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*\xc5\xa1ny - to seed, to cultivate\n (Kogan 2015:571)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The etymology of Proto-MSA *\xc5\xa1ny \xe2\x80\x98to seed, to cultivate\xe2\x80\x99 is uncertain. Har. s\xc3\xa4\xc5\x88i \xe2\x80\x98seed\xe2\x80\x99 (EDH 141), Sel. s\xc3\xa4\xc5\x88e, Wol. s\xc3\xa4\xc5\x88\xc5\x88e and Zwy. s\xc3\xa4\xc5\x88i \xe2\x80\x98seed, crop\xe2\x80\x99 (EDG 555) are in all probability borrowed from Cushitic (Oromo sa\xc5\x88\xc5\x88i, Somali \xc5\xa1uni), but the Cushitic terms, in their turn, may be related to the MSA ones as cognates, thus pointing to a fairly archaic Afroasiatic root completely lost elsewhere in Semitic (Militarev 1999:394). (Kogan 2015:571)\n
