*bl\xe1\xb9\xad - to live, to escape\n (Kogan 2015: 85)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The root pl\xe1\xb9\xad, attested with the meaning \xe2\x80\x9cto escape\xe2\x80\x9d in Ugaritic, Hebrew and Aramaic, must be related in spite of the phonological irregularity. More\r\nproblematic is Arb. flt \xe2\x80\x98to escape\xe2\x80\x99, usually compared as well (Fronzaroli 1965b:250,\r\n263, 267 and elsewhere)\n

\n \n Akkadian\n \n
\n
\n \n bal\xc4\x81\xe1\xb9\xadu - to get well, to recover from sickness; to be alive, to stay alive\n (CAD B 52, AHw. 99)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n Derived words: bal\xc4\x81\xe1\xb9\xadu \xe2\x80\x98life\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD B 46, AHw. 98), bal\xe1\xb9\xadu \xe2\x80\x98alive, safe and sound, surviving, taken alive\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD B 66, AHw. 100), bal\xe1\xb9\xad\xc4\x81nu \xe2\x80\x98alive\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD B 66, AHw. 100), bal\xe1\xb9\xad\xc5\xabtu \xe2\x80\x98state of being alive\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD B 69, AHw. 100); bul\xe1\xb9\xadu \xe2\x80\x98life, lifetime\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD B 311, AHw. 137); muballi\xe1\xb9\xad\xc4\x81nu \xe2\x80\x98person who has kept someone alive\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD M\xe2\x82\x82 157, AHw. 665); muballittu \xe2\x80\x98barrage, weir (in a canal), cage\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD M\xe2\x82\x82 157, AHw. 665); nabla\xe1\xb9\xadu \xe2\x80\x98healing, life-giving\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD N\xe2\x82\x81 25, AHw. 698); tablittu \xe2\x80\x98fodder, victuals\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD T 29, AHw. 1299).

\r\nIs usually compared to Ugr. pl\xe1\xb9\xad, Hbr. pl\xe1\xb9\xad, Syr. pl\xe1\xb9\xad \xe2\x80\x98to escape\xe2\x80\x99 (Fronzaroli 1965b, 250, 263, 267; instead of Arb. flt \xe2\x80\x98to escape\xe2\x80\x99, phonetically remote, cf. rather Arb. b\xc4\x81la\xe1\xb9\xada \xe2\x80\x98to flee\xe2\x80\x99, bulu\xe1\xb9\xad- \xe2\x80\x98fugitives\xe2\x80\x99, LA 7 300).\n
