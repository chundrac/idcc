*\xc5\x9daday- - (cultivated) field\n (Kogan 2015: 307)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The meaning \xe2\x80\x9ccultivated field\xe2\x80\x9d for *\xc5\x9dadaw- is a highly specific PC innovation with no precedent in other Semitic languages, where this concept is expressed, inter alia, by the reflexes of PS *\xe1\xb8\xa5a\xe1\xb8\xb3l- (Fronzaroli 1969 :8\xe2\x80\x929, 26)\n

\n \n *\xc5\x9dadaw- - open country, wild, uncultivated place\n (Fronzaroli 1968: 269\xe2\x80\x92270, 287; Kogan 2011: 190; Kogan 2015: 307)\n \n
\n
\n
\n\n
\n

reconstruction with *-w (rather than -y) seems to be assured by well-attested spellings with -u-/-w- in Sargonic (\xc5\x9ba-d\xc3\xba-e, \xc5\x9ba-d\xc3\xba-im) and Old Assyrian (\xc5\xa1a-ad-wi-im, \xc5\xa1a-du-im), v. Kienast 1994:278\xe2\x80\x92280 and CAD \xc5\xa0\xe2\x82\x81 51 respectively
