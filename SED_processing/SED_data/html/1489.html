Proto-MSA
\n
\n

*prd - to flee in panic\n (Kogan 2015:567\xe2\x80\x93568)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n Leslau plausibly connects Proto-MSA *prd \xe2\x80\x98to flee in panic\xe2\x80\x99 with PS *prd \xe2\x80\x98to be separated,\xe2\x80\x99 represented by Hbr. prd \xe2\x80\x98to separate, to diverge\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 962), Arb. frd \xe2\x80\x98to become sole, single\xe2\x80\x99 (Lane 2363), Sab. frd-m \xe2\x80\x98uniquely, alone\xe2\x80\x99 (SD 46), Tgr. t\xc3\xa4far\xc3\xa4d\xc3\xa4 \xe2\x80\x98to part company as enemies\xe2\x80\x99 (WTS 659). The same shift of meaning took place (presumably independently) in Aramaic (Syr. prad \xe2\x80\x98fugit,\xe2\x80\x99 LSyr. 593), as well as in Akkadian, where par\xc4\x81du \xe2\x80\x98to be fearful, disturbed\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD P 141, AHw. 827) displays a remarkable semantic similarity to the MSA verbs (as duly observed in Huehnergard 1991a:693). (Kogan 2015:567-568)\n
