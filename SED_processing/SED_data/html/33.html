PS
\n
\n

*kabid- - liver\n (SED I No. 141)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n A more general meaning \xe2\x80\x98entrail\xe2\x80\x99 can be reconstructed as PS, alongside with \xe2\x80\x98liver\xe2\x80\x99, on the evidence of Akk., Ugr., Mnd. and Arb.; note the meaning \xe2\x80\x98belly\xe2\x80\x99 in Eth. and Amarna. Derivation of \xe2\x80\x98liver\xe2\x80\x99 from the verbal root \xe2\x80\x98to be heavy\xe2\x80\x99 (*kbd -see, for example, [CDG 273]) is typologically tenable; cf. [ibid.] and [Dolg. BP 268].\n
