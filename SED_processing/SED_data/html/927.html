*nbr - to sit; to stay, to live\n (CDG 383; Kogan 2015: 463)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The etymology of Proto-EthS *nbr \xe2\x80\x98to sit\xe2\x80\x99 is unknown, cf. perhaps Arb. nab\xc5\xabr- = \xcb\x80al-\xcb\x80ist- (LA 5 222). Of some interest may be Akk. wabru \xe2\x80\x98Ortsfremder, Beisasse\xe2\x80\x99 (AHw. 1454), \xe2\x80\x98a type of foreigner\xe2\x80\x99 (CAD U/W 388), the semantic shift from \xe2\x80\x9cto sit\xe2\x80\x9d being illustrated by one of the German translations above as well as by Hbr. t\xc5\x8d\xc5\xa1\xc4\x81b \xe2\x80\x98resident alien, sojourner\xe2\x80\x99 (HALOT 1712).\n
