*\xcb\x80i\xc5\xa1 - to, for\n (Arakelova 2001:29)\n

\n
\n
\n\n
\n
\n The reconstruction is not entirely certain. Akk. i\xc5\xa1 is only attested in a specific group of texts (the archaic liver models from Mari); moreover, it is sometimes considered to be an equivalent of the terminative marker -i\xc5\xa1. Throughout EthS this preposition can only be distinguished as a part of other prepositions, for which one may also suppose other ways of division. See Arakelova 2001:29\xe2\x80\x9330.\n
